# X Competitor Analysis — Inkd Protocol

*Based on direct account observation, content patterns, and engagement signals.*

---

## @jessepollak (Base founder, ~290k followers)

**Posting Frequency:** 2-4 posts/day, 7 days/week. Consistent even on weekends.

**Formats that perform:**
- Build logs ("spent the weekend on X") — high retweets from builders
- Short takes on ecosystem moves (1-3 sentences, no hashtags)
- Lists of builders with what they're building (discovery posts, high engagement)
- Replies to other founders = stays in conversation, algo reward

**What engages:**
- Anything that names real builders and projects (discoverability loop)
- "wild to see X + Y + Z coming together" format — optimism without cringe
- Simple assertions: "Base is going to be the home of onchain agents" — no hedging
- Thread starters that end with "what are you building?" — comment bait

**What doesn't:**
- Long technical deep-dives rarely go wide
- Promotional copy for Base products = low engagement unless tied to a story

**What we steal:**
1. **The builder spotlight format** — name 3 builders building on inkd + what they're doing
2. **Time-framing** — "spent the week looking at X" adds personal authenticity
3. **Simple one-line conviction posts** — state the obvious truth nobody says clearly

## @graphprotocol (The Graph, ~180k followers)

**Posting Frequency:** 1-2 posts/day, weekdays. Quieter weekends.

**Formats that perform:**
- "X is now live on The Graph" — clean, specific, no bloat
- Grant/ecosystem announcements with concrete numbers
- Technical updates with deployment status (testnet → mainnet progression posts)
- "Now indexing X" posts — shows momentum without saying the word momentum

**What engages:**
- New protocol integrations — the crypto community amplifies these
- Concrete developer milestones (subgraph counts, query volume numbers)
- Ecosystem growth data (# of developers, # of queries/month)

**What doesn't:**
- Generic "Web3 is the future" content — no traction
- Vague roadmap teases without dates

**What we steal:**
1. **Integration announcement format** — "X is now using inkd for agent memory" (clean)
2. **Data posts** — "X inscriptions on inkd. Y tokens minted. Z ETH in vault." weekly
3. **Developer milestone posts** — specific, verifiable numbers only

## @ENSdomains (Ethereum Name Service, ~230k followers)

**Posting Frequency:** 1-3 posts/day. Consistent. Mix of proactive and reactive.

**Formats that perform:**
- "your name, now on X chain" — expands reach each integration
- Educational 1-tweet explainers: one concept, explained simply
- Reaction to ecosystem news that relates to naming/identity
- Partnerships where ENS is the foundation layer

**What engages:**
- Anything about identity ownership resonates strongly
- Simple visual infographics get high impressions
- Threads explaining ENS to new users at bull market peaks

**What doesn't:**
- Developer-only technical content
- Governance posts unless major

**What we steal:**
1. **Ownership framing** — ENS owns "your name, your identity." We own "your data, your memory." (But avoid the "your X" anaphora — state it once, not as a pattern)
2. **Simple explainer format** — 1 concept per tweet, no jargon
3. **Integration-as-expansion** — every new agent framework that supports inkd is a "reach" post

## @LitProtocol (Lit Protocol, ~45k followers)

**Formats that perform:**

- "what Lit enables" posts — use cases, not features
- Cross-protocol collaboration announcements
- Build logs from the team

**What engages:**
- The encryption/privacy angle resonates with security-conscious devs

- Posts that don't show what you actually built

2. **Code-in-tweet format** — small SDK examples inline in posts
3. **Cross-protocol angle** — Lit + inkd is a natural pair (access control + storage)

## Top 3 Meta-Insights

**1. Numbers kill vagueness.**
Every account that performs well uses specific numbers. Not "many tokens" — "47,293 tokens." Not "fast" — "3-second finality." The number IS the content.

**2. The best accounts spotlight others.**
@jessepollak names builders. @gitcoin highlights grantees. @graphprotocol celebrates integrations. Accounts that only talk about themselves plateau. Find the builders building on inkd. Spotlight them.

**3. Format diversity beats frequency.**
@LitProtocol posts less than @base but has proportionally higher engagement per post among developers. For a technical protocol at 0→1, 1 great post beats 5 mediocre ones. Quality gate your content ruthlessly.
