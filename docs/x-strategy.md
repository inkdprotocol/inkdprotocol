## Content-Säulen

### 1. Atomic Posts (existing queue — gut, aber timing matters)
Nicht täglich dumpen. 1 Post/Tag maximum outbound. Score 9/10 Posts zuerst.

**Posting-Zeit:** 14:00-16:00 UTC (bestes Engagement in crypto twitter)

### 2. Reactive Replies (das Wichtigste)
Wenn jessepollak, Shaw, oder ein anderer Key Account über agents/ownership postet:
- **Innerhalb 30min replyen** mit einem scharfen 1-2 Satz der alleine steht
- Kein "wir haben das gebaut" — authentisch + informiert klingen
- Ziel: Leute die den Thread lesen sehen @inkdprotocol und think "wer ist das"

**Beispiel-Reply auf jessepollak's "base is the home of onchain agents"-Posts:**
> ownership is the missing layer. agents on base can transact, build, and deploy — but what they create doesn't belong to them until there's a registry. that's what we're building.

### 3. Build Signal (stärkster Differenziator)
Wenn etwas shipped wird (mainnet, npm, subgraph):
- Post mit konkreten Zahlen
- "shipped: [X]. here's what it means for [specific use case]"
- Keine Ankündigungen — nur wenn's live ist

## Erste 10 Posts — Überarbeitet + Priorisiert

Diese ersetzen/ergänzen die queue. Reihenfolge ist wichtig — erzählt eine Geschichte.

---

**POST 1 — Wer sind wir** (erster Post überhaupt)
> agents can hold $10m in a wallet. they can write and deploy contracts. they cannot own a single file without a human co-signing every step. that's the gap. inkd is the registry.

*Warum:* Setzt Kontext, erklärt das Problem UND die Lösung in 2 Sätzen. Für Leute die @inkdprotocol noch nie gesehen haben.

---

**POST 2 — Glaubwürdigkeit** (Tag 2 oder 3)
> 716 tests. 159 contracts. 281 sdk. 276 cli. we wrote tests because agents will depend on this — and agents don't file support tickets.

*Warum:* Zeigt Build-Tiefe sofort. Niemand hat diese Zahlen.

---

**POST 3 — Das Kernprinzip** (Tag 4-5)
> 1 $inkd locked = 1 project registered. permanently. the token doesn't unlock. the project doesn't expire. the registry doesn't go offline. deflationary because it's functional, not financial.

*Warum:* Erklärt den Mechanismus + macht es für token-focused people interesting

---

**POST 4 — Kontrast-Hook** (Tag 6-7)
> github is not a registry. it's a hosting platform. you don't own your repo. you have an account that can be suspended, deleted, or acquired. inkd is what ownership on top of hosting looks like.

*Warum:* Attackiert eine platform die jeder kennt → schafft cognitive dissonance

---

**POST 5 — Stack-Positioning** (Tag 8-9)
> arweave + base + inkd is a stack, not three separate bets. arweave: content lives forever. base: ownership logic runs forever. inkd: the bridge. nobody built this layer before because nobody expected agents to need it.

*Warum:* Positioniert inkd im ecosystem ohne arrogant zu klingen

---

**POST 6 — Philosophisch** (Tag 10)
> the difference between "i built this" and "i can prove i built this" is a transaction hash. one is a claim. the other is evidence.

*Warum:* Kürzester, schärfster Post in der Queue. Perfekt zum RT'en.

---

**POST 7 — Engagement bait** (Tag 12)
> where does your agent store what it built? serious question. we've spoken to 50+ developers and gotten 40 different answers.

*Warum:* Öffnet Konversation. Replies = signal für twitter algorithm.

---

**POST 8 — Hot take** (Tag 14)
> "trustless" is the most abused word in crypto. a system is trustless when you can verify every claim independently. most "trustless" protocols have at least one admin key somewhere. inkd has no pause. no admin. it just runs.

*Warum:* Security-focused devs lieben das. Differenziert von halbgaren protocols.

---

**POST 9 — React-bait** (Tag 15)
> every serious protocol on base solves a layer. uniswap: liquidity. safe: custody. ens: naming. inkd: provenance. they don't compete. they stack.

*Warum:* Gut RT'bar weil es andere mentions implizit beinhaltet

---

**POST 10 — Zukunft** (Tag 17-18)
> the next IP dispute isn't between two companies. it's between a company and an agent that built something on their servers using their api with their data. courts aren't ready. neither is github. we are.

*Warum:* Forward-looking, schafft urgency, unique take
